Transparent custodianship increases trust in registers

1 What is custodianship?

Custodianship is the process of maintaining a list by a group, institution or individual.

Read more about custodianship

2 What is a register?

A register is a list that lots of people care about which an institution has promised to keep accurate and complete, and has a clear enough governance that people trust it enough to reuse it.

Read more about registers

3 How do you get people to trust a list?

People trust lists with good custodianship.

A large part of custodianship is ensuring the accuracy and completeness of your list by following best practices for publishing.

Custodianship is also the set of processes and tools that govern entries in the list.

For many lists, improving the transparency that surrounds these processes is the simplest and cheapest way to increase trust in their contents.

4 What makes transparent custodianship?

Transparent custodianship of a list means being clear and open about the following things

Who maintains the list and why?

Which individual, group or institution is responsible for making sure it remains accurate and complete?
