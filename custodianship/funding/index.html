Who is paying for this?

The practice of ensuring that a list remains accurate and complete over time requires work. This often requires funding to pay for staff or infrastructure.

Understanding who pays for this can provide us with valuable insights into the set of incentive mechanisms and behaviours which will surround it, as well as allowing us to make informed judgements about how we may choose to trust it.

State funding

When the funding comes from a state, custodians can describe this using language such as

The custodianship of this register is funded by the Cabinet Office.

Revenue generation

When the funding comes from a revenue generation, custodians can describe this using language such as

HM Land Registry is a non-ministerial government department which makes no call on funds voted by parliament. By statute HM Land Registry is required to ensure that income from fees charged to change or query the register cover expenditure under normal operating conditions.

Grant funding

When the funding comes from avenues such as grant funding, custodians can describe this using language such as

The custodianship of this list is provided by Democracy Club. This work is done by volunteers and paid staff who are funded by philanthropic grants.
